## Overview
This project analyzes automotive data to uncover market trends, performance metrics, and safety insights across various car manufacturers and models. Using Python data science tools and a comprehensive car dataset, the analysis highlights industry-leading features, performance outliers, and key buyer decision factors.

## Contents
- **Automotive-Trend-Analysis.ipynb:** Python notebook with all analysis and visualizations.
- **DS1_C4_S9_Car_Data.xlsx:** Raw dataset of automotive specs, features, and ratings.
- **DS1_C4_S9_Car_Data-Dictionary.xlsx:** Data dictionary explaining every field.

## Key Analyses & Insights

- **Performance Metrics:**  
  - Evaluated displacement, cylinder configuration, and valve counts across car brands.
  - Ranked manufacturers on fuel efficiency and top engine specs.

- **Safety Analysis:**  
  - Identified top manufacturers prioritizing ABS, Hill Assist, and airbags.
  - Filtered cars and brands that meet modern safety standards and regulatory compliance.

- **Comfort & Features:**  
  - Compared seating capacity and child safety lock prevalence for family-friendly vehicles.
  - Analyzed central locking systems and premium comfort features.

- **Body & Dimensions:**  
  - Calculated average body volumes by manufacturer, highlighting trends among SUVs, sedans, and city cars.
  - Assessed cars for size segmentationâ€”large-volume luxury/SUVs vs compact city models.

- **Weight & Load:**  
  - Generated stats on kerb and gross vehicle weight to compare robustness across brands.
  - Identified weight outliers and typical vehicle profiles.

- **Data Cleaning & Outlier Handling:**  
  - Imputed missing values, standardized measurements, and visualized distribution outliers.
  - Implemented boxplots and correlation matrices for key quantitative features.

- **Trend Visualizations:**  
  - Interactive tables, charts, and boxplots enable dynamic insights on market leaders and feature distribution.

## Getting Started

1. Download or clone the repository.
2. Open `Automotive-Trend-Analysis.ipynb` in Jupyter/Colab.
3. Ensure the required data files (`DS1_C4_S9_Car_Data.xlsx`, `DS1_C4_S9_Car_Data-Dictionary.xlsx`) are in the root directory.
4. Run notebook cells to reproduce all analysis and visualizations.
